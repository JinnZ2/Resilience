# Game Theory Axiom Failure Proofs

13 formal proofs demonstrating that game theory's foundational axioms fail
under conditions that exist in real systems. All proofs verified valid.

## The Scraper Principle

where the game-theoretic "rational" strategy (stay home, let someone else
clear the road) produces system failure, while the actual behavior that
saved the situation (one person going out with a truck and a scraper)
cannot be derived from any Nash equilibrium.

## Proof Methods

The 13 proofs use four complementary formal systems:

1. **Aristotelian Logic** — Classical syllogistic reasoning showing internal contradictions
2. **Set Theory** — Axiom violations in the formal structure
3. **Evolutionary Mathematics** — Fitness landscape analysis showing that "rational" strategies are evolutionarily unstable
4. **Scraper Principle** — Physical-reality grounding showing axiom failure in survival scenarios

## Why This Matters

Game theory is embedded in:
- Economic policy models
- Military strategy (including autonomous weapons targeting)
- AI alignment approaches (RLHF reward modeling)
- Institutional design
- Treaty and negotiation frameworks

If the axioms fail — which these proofs demonstrate they do — then
systems built on those axioms produce systematically wrong predictions
in exactly the conditions (cascading crises, survival scenarios,
commons problems) where correct predictions matter most.

## Connection to Repository

The food system model shows a system where "rational" individual responses
(substitute to cheapest calories, reduce planting after bad year) produce
collective catastrophe. The Scraper Principle explains WHY: the behavior
that maintains system viability cannot be derived from individual utility
maximization.

The corridor's 7 knowledge holders are all "scrapers" — people whose
behavior is inexplicable under game theory but essential for system survival.
