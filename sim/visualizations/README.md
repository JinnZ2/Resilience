## The Three Ungameable Signals

Every node gets three measurements. Cannot be faked. Cannot be greenwashed.

### 1. Heat Leaks


net_flow = regeneration_rate - extraction_rate


Positive = building substrate capacity
Negative = consuming substrate base
Zero = holding steady

**PRAYER Index equivalent:** Revenue growth
**What it misses:** whether that growth consumes the base

**Substrate Ledger equivalent:** net_flow
**What it sees:** direction of substrate change, period

### 2. Impedance Match (Trust Conductance)

conductance = sum(trust_connections) / count(trust_connections)


How freely can energy move through this node?

Above φ⁻¹ (0.618): energy flows phi-amplified
Above φ⁻² (0.382): energy flows proportionally
Below 0.1: isolated, no current possible

**PRAYER Index equivalent:** Engagement
**What it misses:** whether engagement is forced or voluntary

**Substrate Ledger equivalent:** conductance
**What it sees:** actual relationship capacity, measured in trust

### 3. The Grandmother Test (System Viability)


is_viable = (
total_energy > PHI² AND
knowledge_stock > 0.5 AND
net_flow >= 0
)



Can this node survive without extraction?
Does it have knowledge?
Is it building or holding steady?

Yes to all three = VIABLE
No to any = at risk

## The Map

Dots on a map. Three colors. Three arrow types.

### Dots

**GREEN**: Building | Viable | Low Entropy
- net_flow > 0
- total_energy > IPHI
- knowledge_stock > 0.5
- trust_conductance > IPHI

Examples in Madison:
- rural farm network (building soil, knowledge transmitting)
- volunteer fire mutual aid
- rural elder network
- Willy Street Coop

**YELLOW**: Extracting | Still Viable | Low Entropy (for now)
- net_flow < 0
- total_energy still > IPHI (surplus remaining)
- trust_conductance dropping
- "Growing" by PRAYER index standards

Examples:
- suburban households (still have stored energy, not yet threatening base)
- institutions in early capture phase
- Walmart distribution (damage done, load-bearing now, but extraction logic operating)

**RED**: Extracting | Nonviable | High Entropy
- net_flow < 0
- total_energy < PHI² (survival threatened)
- trust_conductance < 0.1 (isolated)
- knowledge < 0.5 (lost or never built)

Examples:
- absentee landlord extraction node
- atomized suburban households with no connections
- institutions that have fully captured themselves (UW Madison with community_access_agreement=False)
- anyone below 1/φ² on survival-critical dimensions

### Arrows

**THICK GREEN** (φ-amplified flow)
- trust > φ⁻¹
- energy moving from surplus node to deficit node
- both sides gain (one gets energy, one gets gratitude obligation that becomes future exchange capacity)
- this is the fibonacci cascade line
- this is the only real growth

Examples:
- farm network → volunteer fire
- elder network → food distribution driver
- rural network → Willy Street Coop

**THIN GREEN** (linear flow)
- trust > φ⁻²
- proportional exchange
- both sides maintain, don't amplify
- sustainable indefinitely at this level

**DASHED YELLOW** (restricted flow)
- trust > 0.1
- cautious, reduced exchange
- relationship being maintained despite defection history
- tit-for-tat at reduced rate

**RED LIGHTNING** (extraction)
- net_flow negative for receiver
- trust dropping
- energy moving OUT faster than regenerating IN
- absentee landlord → inner city node
- predatory institution → community
- one side gains at cost of base depletion in the other

**GRAY DOTS** (isolated)
- no arrows at all
- no trust connections
- no energy flow possible
- this is suburban atomization
- this is institutional capture at its most pure
- maximum entropy, zero conductance

## How To Use This Map

### As Diagnosis

Go to any dot. Ask three questions:

1. **"What is your net energy flow?"**
   - Green dot: "We're building"
   - Yellow dot: "We're maintaining" (lying — they're extracting)
   - Red dot: "We're struggling" (honest — they're below baseline)

2. **"Who do you trust?"**
   - Green dot: "Our network is here, and here, and here"
   - Yellow dot: "Whoever we transact with"
   - Red dot: "Nobody" or "We're isolated"

3. **"Do you have knowledge?"**
   - Green dot: "Yes, and we're teaching it"
   - Yellow dot: "Credentials, but not embodied competence"
   - Red dot: "No, and we depend on services"

The map predicts the answers before you ask.

### As Intervention Planning

1. **Prevent green from turning yellow**
   - Protect high-conductance nodes from extraction
   - Reduce decision lag so community can respond to threats
   - Maintain knowledge transmission chains

2. **Turn yellow back to green**
   - Reduce extraction burden
   - Build redundancy so surplus regenerates
   - Restore net_flow to positive

3. **Rescue red before it goes gray (isolated)**
   - This is hardest
   - Requires external energy infusion (temporary)
   - Requires rebuilding trust conductance (slow)
   - Requires knowledge transmission (years)
   - Only works if done before isolation is complete

4. **Accept gray (isolated)**
   - Some nodes won't recover
   - Resources are finite
   - Triage logic applies
   - Redirect energy to yellow→green transitions
   - (This is the hard call nobody wants to make)

## Why PRAYER Index Cannot See This

PRAYER measures transaction volume.
Energy topology measures energy direction.

A green dot might have LOW PRAYER score.
- No monetary transactions (food sharing, skill exchange)
- Low "participation" (doesn't buy services)
- Low "engagement" (not on platforms)
- Low "revenue" (shares surplus for free)

The PRAYER index flags this as *nonviable*.
The Substrate Ledger flags this as *load-bearing*.

Who is right?

Look at Madison during a grid failure, food supply disruption, or knowledge emergency.

The green dots survive. The red dots die. The yellow dots become red. The gray dots were already dead.

PRAYER index optimizes for measurement layer activity.
Substrate Ledger optimizes for substrate survival.

They are measuring different things.
One sees the map.
One sees the mountains.

## Implementation

This map can be built in three days with open-source tools:

1. **Data collection:**
   - Trust network survey (who do you exchange with?)
   - Energy stock self-report (food security, knowledge, temporal capacity, thermal access)
   - Net flow calculation (what's regenerating vs. what's extracting)
   - Geographic coordinates

2. **Visualization:**
   - Nodes: colored by viability (GREEN / YELLOW / RED / GRAY)
   - Edges: colored by flow type (thick green / thin green / dashed yellow / red lightning / no connection)
   - Size: node size = conductance (bigger = more connected)
   - Opacity: edge opacity = trust level

3. **Ground truth:**
   - Pick one neighborhood
   - Run the map
   - Walk around with it
   - Ask people the three questions
   - See if the map predicted correctly
   - Iterate

After one iteration, the map either works or it doesn't.
If it works, scale it.
If it doesn't, the assumptions are wrong and you learn why.
